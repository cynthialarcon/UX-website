How I got started in UX design while being a Product Owner

Working as a PO helped me to understand the life cycle management of a product and to see right through my eyes the importance of the user in the development of the product.

I have 3 years of experience in IT industry and almost a year as a product owner. I have seen how stakeholders feed their requirements into the product design forgetting about the user.

3 key ingredients of any successful product

  1. Viability => Money - has to make/save money for the company.
  2. Feasibility => Technology - has to be buildable by someone at a price that makes it viable and in a timeframe that is reasonable.
  3. Desirability => Someone needs or wants this product - should solve an issue or else it shouldnâ€™t exist. When it is used it should create a positive feeling that makes them want to use it again.

Many companies look at the first 2, but not the 3rd.That's why you see so many products that are viable and feasible but nobody needs or wants to use or enjoys using it.

UX solves these issues - that's why I got in love with it!

My goal as a UX Designer

I want to make machines behave better, to be polite, to be nicer, to expect humans to make mistakes, to expect them to be slow and not to punish them for these human qualities. To be more considerate and understanding instead.
