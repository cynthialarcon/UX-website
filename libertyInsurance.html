Renewing car insurance

Client: Liberty Insurance Ireland


THE PROBLEM

Liberty Insurance can not offer the service to renew a car policy online at the moment, and they are failing at guiding the user on how to proceed with the process.

Liberty Insurance provides vehicles and home insurance. At the moment, June 2020, the service to renew a car policy is not available, the reasons are unknown to me. The problem is that the website is failing to guide the user on alternative actions to proceed and conclude with renewing their car policy. The objective of this study is to explore how Liberty Insurance can communicate alternative ways to contact them and finish the process of renewing car insurance even if their online system doesn't work.
The plan was to conduct usability testing and surveys to understand the user, pain points and behaviours while renewing car insurance.
The end result is a medium fidelity prototype that was tested with users.  



MY RESPONSABILITIES

  • User Research
  • Usability testing and survey
  • User flow
  • Low and medium fidelity prototypes

MAIN GOAL

Design a solution that provides the user with alternative ways to contact Liberty Insurance team to conclude renewing their car insurance.

TIMELINE

One week sprint:
  • Monday: Map out the problem and pick an important place to focus.
  • Tuesday: Sketch competing solutions on paper.
  • Wednesday: Make difficult decisions and turn ideas into a testable hypothesis.
  • Thursday: Hammer out a medium-fidelity prototype.
  • Friday: Test it with real live humans.

Understand
the user

Conducted usability testing and a survey to understand the user, the goals, behaviours, environment and pain points.

The first thing to do was to set up some users and ask them to attempt to renew their car policy insurance. I chose to ask them to perform the following task on the website:

  • Show me how would you renew your car insurance.

Survey results

Executed a survey with 13 users to get an idea of what are their behaviours and expectations while renewing on a website.

Users that renew online, do it because:

  • Get the best discounts
  • Ease of managing the paperwork and making payment
  • Cheap prices
  • Easy method
  • Easy/ quick and simple
  • Simple and environmentally friendly
  • It's convenient

Based on the research I could define the following

Users

Working adults living in Ireland, owners of a car.

Goals

As an owner of a car
I want to renew my insurance policy
So that I protect myself from any accident.

As a working person
I want to renew my policy online
So that the process is easy, quick, simple
and eases the paperwork and payment.

Environment

Home or work,or home office.
This might not be the only paperwork a user would do within a year, they might be dealing with a mortgage, claiming taxes, health insurance, and so on.

Behaviours

Users would do this once every year and with 2 weeks in advance before their policy expires.

Pain points

The usability tests showed the following pain points
1

If the user tries to renew online, after inserting their details they’d get the following text: “Sorry but this service is not available. Click Here if you would like to get a new quote.”

2

After getting the previous message the page still shows a “Continue” button. If the user presses that, it’d throw an error asking to enter a policy number and a day of birth. So, it ends in a loop.

3

“Manage my policy” and “Help center” pages don’t help as they redirect the user to the same “original” page that has the issue. So, by targeting pain point 1, this would disappear.

"Click here" button leads to confusion

The page shows that the service is not available and the link provided leads to the Home page leading the user to confusion.

Home page links don't help either

Once on the home page, there are few other links that the user might think could help: “Manage my policy” and “Help center”. Unfortunately, they don't help, as they point to the same page with the same issues

Continue button - an infinite loop

Takes the user into an infinite loop that leads nowhere and frustrates the user.

Concept design

Based on the research done I could identify the following use cases and set up some assumptions for this sprint.

Identified 1 use case:

  • I want to renew my car policy online

Some assumptions:

  • The user has a policy number or a vehicle registration
  • The company can provide phone assistance : Monday — Friday 8am — 7pm and Saturday 8am — 4pm. Phone number is: 1890 944 412
  • The company can provide Live chat: Monday — Friday 8am — 7pm.

Making decisions to target pain points

Pain point 1 is the biggest issue, by targetting it pain point 3 would disappear.
1

If the user tries to renew online, after inserting their details they’d get the following text: “Sorry but this service is not available. Click Here if you would like to get a new quote.”

Changes described below.

2

After getting the previous message the page still shows a “Continue” button.

This button has to disappear once the system shows that the service is not available.

3

“Manage my policy” and “Help center” pages don’t help as they redirect the user to the same “original” page that has the issue.

So, by targeting pain point 1, this would disappear and provide correct information.

Fixing pain point 1:

  • "Sorry but this service is not available”
    This statement is good but I will go further and apologize on behalf of the team. The text would change to: “Apologies for the inconvenience, this service is not available at the moment”
  • “Click Here if you would like to get a new quote.”
    This statement has to be removed and provide other ways to contact the team. The company offers customer service on phone and online, via a chat so I will add these 2 options.

New user flow

Clear message

If the company for whatever reason can not provide an online service, the message when trying to do so has to be clear and provide the users with an alternative solution.

Alternative options to contact Liberty Insurance

  1. Phone call (which is a popular option in Liberty Insurance).
  2. Live chat (which they also provide).

Email option could be given but I decided to limit it to 2 options, as more options the user has, the more decisions they have to take.

Successful policy renewal

By communicating that the user has 2 options to contact the team, the user can then decide which way to go and talk to an agent, this will end up in a successful policy renewal.

Lessons learned

 What did I learn from this journey?
  1. I am still fascinated on the work that can be done in one week sprint.
  2. Iteration is key, things will never be correct after 1st or 2nd iteration. The more I iterate, the better results I can have.
  3. I was thrilled to have found a solution to the input problem. In my previous case study I didn’t know how to add an input field (so I did it for the user) but this time I decided I would find the solution. I found Framer and a package called “Input” from Benjamin den Boer that does the job! Using this package makes the prototype more real and the user can interact with it. The issue I had now, is that it can accept +2 characters where I don't need it so I will find out if I can restrict that.
  4. In my usability testing, I was conscious of not jumping straight away to reply (like in my previous usability testing) or ask something, leaving more room to the user to speak up and it worked! I was surprised by how much information I got from just one question. I definitely need to keep doing this.
  5. This time I created the prototype in Framer and I liked it! the learning curve is fast for the basic so I will continue using it! :)
